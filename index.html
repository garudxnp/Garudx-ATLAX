1.0 Executive Abstract

The operational theater of the high Himalayas presents a logistical singularity where traditional rotorcraft efficacy degrades asymptotically due to rarefied air density. This paper validates the GarudX ATLAX, a heavy-lift Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) engineered to overcome these physics constraints.

Utilizing a 28S (103.6V nominal) high-voltage architecture driving 57-inch carbon fiber coaxial rotors, the system achieves a measured peak thrust of 452 kg (113 kg/axis) at sea level. Powered by a 66,000 mAh semi-solid state energy matrix with a specific energy of 350 Wh/kg, the platform is modeled to deliver a 30 kg payload to Camp 2 (6,400m) with a 20% energy reserve. The analysis integrates bench-test data of the H13 propulsion system with ISA atmospheric modeling to confirm feasibility.

4.0 Atmospheric Physics Modeling

To rigorously model lift at 6,500m, we must derive the air density function $\rho(h)$ from first principles, integrating the hydrostatic equation through the troposphere's thermal gradient.

4.1 Hydrostatic Equilibrium Condition
$$ \frac{dP}{dh} = -\rho g $$

The pressure gradient $dP/dh$ balances the weight of the air column ($\rho g$).

4.2 Ideal Gas Law Substitution
$$ \rho = \frac{P}{R T} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{dP}{dh} = -\frac{P g}{R T(h)} $$
$$ \frac{dP}{P} = -\frac{g}{R T(h)} dh $$
4.3 Temperature Lapse Integration

In the troposphere, $T(h) = T_0 - Lh$. We integrate from $h=0$ to $h$:

$$ \int_{P_0}^{P} \frac{dP'}{P'} = -\frac{g}{R} \int_{0}^{h} \frac{dh'}{T_0 - Lh'} $$
$$ \ln\left(\frac{P}{P_0}\right) = \frac{g}{RL} \ln\left(1 - \frac{Lh}{T_0}\right) $$
4.4 Final Density Function

Solving for P and substituting back into the Ideal Gas Law yields the governing density equation:

$$ \rho(h) = \rho_0 \left( 1 - \frac{L h}{T_0} \right)^{\left(\frac{g}{R L} - 1\right)} $$

5.0 Rotor Dynamics & Power Theory

Validation of heavy lift at 6,500m requires calculating the Shaft Power ($P_{shaft}$) needed to generate Thrust ($T$). We utilize Momentum Theory extended with Bernoulli's principle.

5.1 Bernoulli's Principle (Pressure Delta)

Across the rotor disk, pressure jumps from $P_u$ (under) to $P_l$ (lower). Far upstream/downstream pressure is $P_\infty$.

$$ \Delta P = \frac{1}{2} \rho v_{wake}^2 $$

Thrust is pressure delta times area: $T = A \Delta P$. Thus $T = \frac{1}{2} \rho A v_{wake}^2$.

5.2 Induced Velocity Relation

Momentum conservation states $T = \dot{m} v_{wake}$, where $\dot{m} = \rho A v_i$. In hover, $v_{wake} = 2v_i$.

$$ v_i = \sqrt{\frac{T}{2 \rho A}} $$
5.3 Induced Power (Ideal)
$$ P_{induced} = T \cdot v_i = \frac{T^{3/2}}{\sqrt{2 \rho A}} $$

CRITICAL: $P_{induced}$ scales with $1/\sqrt{\rho}$. At 6,500m ($\rho \approx 0.53\rho_0$), power increases by $\sim 37\%$.

5.4 Total Electrical Power Calculation

Including Profile Power ($P_0$) and Motor Efficiency ($\eta_m$).

$$ P_{elec} = \frac{1}{\eta_m} \left( \kappa_{coax} \frac{T^{3/2}}{\sqrt{2 \rho A}} + \frac{\sigma C_{d0}}{8} \rho A (\Omega R)^3 \right) $$

16.0 Technical Conclusion

The updated engineering analysis, incorporating empirical data from the H13 108V propulsion tests, confirms the GarudX ATLAX is viable for high-altitude heavy-lift operations.

The bench data validates that a 4-axis coaxial system can generate over 450 kg of thrust at sea level. At 6,500m ASL, correcting for air density ($\rho \approx 0.53 \rho_0$), the system retains approximately 240 kg of peak thrust capacity. This provides a robust safety factor of 2.4x for a 100 kg mission AUW, ensuring control authority in Himalayan wind shear conditions. The 6.8 kWh energy matrix provides sufficient endurance for the Base Camp to Camp 2 operational cycle.
